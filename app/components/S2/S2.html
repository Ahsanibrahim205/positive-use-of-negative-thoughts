What is Positive use of Negative thoughts?

  • Research shows that if you just try to tell yourself to stop thinking negative thoughts they will probably show themselves more intensely and more frequently. How strange!
  • For example if you tell yourself:
    1. I dare not, I must not serve badly this morning as my opponent has a great return and he will clobber me”, this actually increases the chances that you will serve less well than normal.
    2. “I had better not make a mistake when presenting these facts and figures as my boss will be in the meeting”; there will be an increased probability that you will make a mistake.
    3. “Under no circumstances should I be tense. I must relax”; this leads to an increased likelihood that you will become tenser.
  • Similarly, by trying to conceal or bottle up or fight emotions such as fear, anger, frustration and helplessness, you are more likely to dwell upon them and intensify the feelings.
  • Remember and accept that it is actually all right to experience and let out some negative emotions in a positive, controlled manner. Frequently just naming the feelings can reduce their impact.
